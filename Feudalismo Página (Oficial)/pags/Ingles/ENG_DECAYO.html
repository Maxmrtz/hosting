How and When Decayed?
  • In the 16th century, a new industrial production system took root in Europe, which will change the nature of society very quickly.

  • Developed pur the pequenus campesinus the feudal exploitation. To the extent that these struggles were successful and meant some liberation. an accumulation of capital will be produced in the hands of the peasant, thus appearing the bourgeoisie and an endogenous ecanism towards capitalism.

  • These tendencies and phenomena will be supported by the development of trade and production, but above all the development of the cities.

  • To the extent that the process of decomposition of the feudal system is accelerated, a differentiation between the rich and the rich will begin to emerge, and bourgeois production relations will begin to appear, and this is the case because, little by little, the rich peasants became contract workers.

  • Some authors introduce as one of the key factors of the end of feudalism the discovery of distance from the New World and with long trade.

  • In other nations, such as France, the old nobility was stronger for a longer period of time, since the state protected the interests of the landowning nobility until the international competition brought the economic crisis to an extreme. The political and economic crisis led to a violent revolution and the class of merchants (bourgeoisie) emerged as dominant.
