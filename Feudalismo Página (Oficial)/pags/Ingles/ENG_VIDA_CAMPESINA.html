What did they do and how did they live?

In spite of the excellent life that the feudal ones could obtain, the farmers and workers offered their work arduously to be able to cover their basic needs, for example:


  • The personal benefit. They had the commitment to work the lands of the fiefdom for at least three days and the rest of the time they destined it to the lands for their economy. This meant that the farmer worked all week in the lands of others, and if he wanted to promote their economy, they had to spend the nights or Sundays to work their own lands.

  • Rent in kind. This rent system consisted of the peasant having to provide the feudal lord with certain quantities of wheat or certain quantities of animals or birds, which would be the tribute they paid for the granting of land.

  • Rent of money. By increasing production it was necessary to create a new rent system, which would allow the feudal lord to keep the fief. As agricultural production and artisanal production grew, citizens had to pay taxes and taxes for production.
