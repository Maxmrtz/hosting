Their Technological Advances

Within the feudal period there were varied changes for agricultural production, except for the scientific. But then we will learn some:

The invasions suffered by Europe for more than a hundred years (norms, Muslims, slaves) with the fall of the Roman Empire and the subsequent weakening of the Carolingian empire, slowed down economic activity until Doors of the year 1000.

At this time when it extends modern agricultural techniques. These include the increase in the use of water mills as a driving force and irrigation ditches, extending crops and releasing labor. In addition, improving the methods of engaging animals, especially the horse and the ox, whose breeding increases in a remarkable way and will allow to dispose of draught animals in abundance. Instruments of agricultural use, such as plough or hoe, generally of wood, are replaced by others of iron.

The technical advances allowed demographic growth to be considerable, while 90% of the population lived on the land and were peasants under the command of a lord, the stately domain was increaseded and the peasants were under their control.

Other sectors achieved technological progress, such as glass and different metallurgy, thanks to the development of the blast furnace, while the textile sector developed with the lathe and the Batan. Shipbuilding techniques progressed and were built towards 1300 vessels such as the galleys, which kept communicated to Italy with northeastern Europe, while the large Basque and Catalan freighters sailed the Atlantic to the Azores and Canary islands.
