Social Composition

Feudalism was composted by a determinate number of villages and small-towns, underneath the command and domain from the feudal lords. . The society in the feudal regime used to be divided in two, on the one hand, the feudal lords, who were the dominating and exploiting class, made up the nobility and the clergy, and, on the other hand, the peasants and artisans, who were the working class.

Within the hierarchical class structure of the feudal system, kings occupied the highest and most important position, followed by barons and nobles, clergy and bishops, knights or vassals, and villagers or peasants. The division of classes within the hierarchy of the feudal system was quite marked between the noble classes and the villagers.
